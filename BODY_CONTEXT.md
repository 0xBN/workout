## Hips & Lower Back

### Left QL — Chronic Issue
- Slight scoliosis diagnosed in elementary school, confirmed mild by family doctor
- Chiro recently confirmed: curve is convex right, meaning the **left side is compressed and contracted**, right side is stretched/loose
- Left QL is chronically contracted — it never fully releases because the curve loads it structurally
- Upper back had multiple knots from desk posture, mostly resolved through chiro
- Gets lateral slouching when fatigued — has body awareness to catch it
- QL butt walks daily habit (at home, informal)

### Scoliosis-Specific Protocol
- McGill side bridge: **right side only** — strengthens the convex side to counteract the curve
- QL foam roll: **left side focus** — lateral to spine, between last rib and hip crest
- Standing QL stretch: **left side** — arm overhead, lean right
- Copenhagen plank: bilateral adductor strengthening, pelvic stability
- **Never load asymmetrically in ways that reinforce the curve**

## Upper Body

### Right Shoulder — Chronic Limitation
- Injury from wrestling: thrown and landed wrong, thought it was soreness, healed with permanent restriction
- Restricted movement: **shoulder internal rotation only** — the hands-behind-back / arrested position
- All other positions feel fine and pain-free
- Overhead press is comfortable
- Diagnosis inference: likely posterior capsule tightness from traumatic landing
- **Avoid**: bench press loads horizontal adduction and internal rotation — use overhead press instead for antagonist pressing
- **Helpful**: cross-body stretch, sleeper stretch for posterior capsule
- Slowly improving over time

### Left Ring Finger — Active Recovery
- PIP joint injury — likely A2 pulley or joint capsule strain
- Currently 95% recovered
- **Crimping with left hand avoided** — open hand positions are fine
- Pain occurs specifically on crimping, not on three-finger drag or open hand
- Abrahangs twice daily are the primary rehab and prehab tool
- **Protocol: no crimping left hand until 100% and sustained for 2-3 weeks. Then cautious reintroduction on easy problems only**

## Training Implications

1. Abrahangs are the single highest ROI activity — finger tendon health is the V12 bottleneck
2. Glute activation work is therapeutic for the right knee, not just fitness
3. Overhead press is safer than bench press given right shoulder restriction
4. Pistol squat and eccentric step-down are joint health exercises, not just strength work
5. All scoliosis corrections are right-side biased (McGill bridge, QL work)
6. Post-stretch compliance improves when the routine is under 7 minutes
7. Barefoot movement patterns are an asset — preserve this with foot-friendly exercise selection
